Spring Foraging · 2026
Morel Mushrooms
Morchella · Wild-foraged
What They Are
Honeycomb-capped wild mushrooms that emerge for just a few weeks each spring. Earthy, nutty, and deeply savory.
On the Plate
Sautéed in butter and shallots, folded into pasta, or simmered into cream sauce over a steak.
Wild Ramps
Allium tricoccum · Wild leek
What They Are
Wild leeks foraged from Appalachian forest floors. Garlicky, oniony, slightly funky — bulb to leaf, every part is edible.
On the Plate
Grilled whole, blitzed into pesto, pickled for the bar, or laid across a wood-fired pizza.
Fiddlehead Ferns
Matteuccia struthiopteris · Ostrich fern
What They Are
Tightly coiled fern shoots harvested before they unfurl. Grassy and green — asparagus crossed with spinach. A two-week window each spring.
On the Plate
Always cooked, never raw. Blanched and sautéed with garlic and lemon, or tossed into spring risotto.
Rhubarb
Rheum rhabarbarum · Stalks only
What It Is
Crimson stalks with a sharp, tart bite. Treated as a fruit, technically a vegetable. Leaves are toxic — only the stalk goes to the kitchen.
On the Plate
Stewed into compote, baked into tarts and crisps, or syruped for the bar in a spring spritz.
