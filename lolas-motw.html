The Story

Soft shells are a fleeting spring ritual on the Chesapeake. Blue crabs molt their hard shells for a few short weeks, and for that brief window the entire crab is edible — sweet, briny, and crisp when fried right. Miss the season and you wait another year.

We dredge them light, fry them hot, and let the crab do the work. Underneath: a tangle of frisée and petite watercress dressed in white balsamic vinaigrette, with pickled shallots, watermelon radish, and brioche croutons for crunch. Sauce gribiche — chopped egg, mustard, capers, cornichon, fresh herbs — finishes the plate.

The Season · Chesapeake Bay
Soft shells run from May into early summer. Watermen check their floats by hand around the clock — once a crab molts, it has only hours before the new shell hardens. What you taste is the catch from yesterday.
