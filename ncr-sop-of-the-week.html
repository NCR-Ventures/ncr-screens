Fresh Produce Display
Every bowl. Every shift. Every location.
SOP-030
Effective 2026
THE STANDARD
Vibrant, mounded bowls of fresh fruit at the host stand, every bar, and the chef counter — daily. Color sells. Freshness signals everything we are.
The Look
Lemons · Limes · Oranges
What Goes Where
Host Stand
One large bowl of vibrant yellow lemons. The first thing the guest sees.
Every Bar
Three bowls — lemons, limes, oranges. Each in its own white ceramic bowl on the back bar.
Chef Counter
Heirloom tomatoes in season — mix colors and sizes. Switch to artichokes when tomatoes are out.
Kitchen Counter
Tomatoes, artichokes, citrus, peppers, or seasonal produce in use — one feature, visible to the guest.
Specs & Routine
Stickers
No stickers. Ever. A sticker in the bowl makes the whole display read careless.
Lemon Size
80–120 count. Smaller than 120 = too small.
Freshness
No dried citrus. No mold. No soft fruit. Pull immediately.
Stack
Mound above the rim. Half-full = restock, not display.
False Bottom
Linen napkin allowed under fruit — never visible from side or top.
Open
Bartender owns the bar bowls. Host owns the host stand. Manager owns all others.
Service
Bartenders pull from display bowls. Top off as the bowl draws down.
Close
Pull anything that won't survive overnight. Cover or refrigerate.
Ownership & Billing
Host Stand Bowl Host — checks at open, restocks throughout shift
Bar Bowls Opening Bartender — checks at open, top off through service, closing bartender pulls
All Other Bowls Manager on Duty — chef counter, kitchen, anywhere else
Bowl Replacement GM — chipped or cracked bowls replaced same day
Lemon Allowance 1 case/week credited to Flower & Decor on the produce invoice
