NCR Ventures 3 Things We Did Right Easter Week — April 5, 2026

1
The Floor Held on the Hardest Day 🙌
Easter is a stress test. Full houses, special menus, guests with big expectations. Our servers and hosts delivered across the board. Guests noticed. They wrote names. They said they're coming back. That's a culture win — not a luck win.
2
The Food Is Getting Called Out by Name 🍝
Stroganoff. Braised beef. Carpaccio. Lasagna. Lobster bisque. Veal parm. Carrot cake. Nonna's Sausage Bread. Guests are naming dishes in their reviews. The kitchen is cooking with care and consistency. BOH had a strong week.
3
First-Timers Are Converting 💚
Multiple guests this week said "first time, not my last." That's the flywheel working. One great experience turns a stranger into a regular. The team earned that this week.
NCR Ventures 3 Opportunities to Be Better Easter Week — April 5, 2026
5 of 6
1
Course Sequencing Needs to Be a Hard Rule ⏱️
Entrees before salads. Bread with apps instead of before them. It showed up in multiple reviews this week. Guests feel it even when they can't name it. This gets fixed in pre-shift — not on the fly.
2
Happy Hour Has a Communication Gap 💬
A guest counted her noodles. She felt misled on portion size and didn't know which tables qualified. One sentence from the server before ordering fixes this. We need a system — right now there isn't one.
3
Manager Presence During Recovery Is the Difference ⚡
When things went wrong — wrong courses, overcooked food, a gluten-free mix-up — the manager response was slow or too soft. "Sorry" is not recovery. Real recovery is visible, fast, and personal. Managers need to catch problems before guests flag them.
