NCR Ventures 3 Opportunities to Be Better 🌷 Week Ending 5.3 — Mother's Day Coming In Hot 🌷
5 of 6
1
The Host Stand Is the Front Door
A regular called for a 4-top outside, was told "first come, first served." Drove over with out-of-town guests. Hosts chatting behind the podium. Told 1.5 hour wait. He stepped outside and saw three empty tables. "It is what it is, I was told." That's a 1-star Google review and a regular we may have lost. Read every reservation. Look up. Engage. Hosts are the first hospitality moment of the night — not the last hurdle.
2
"No" Without Trying Is Always the Wrong Answer
A guest asked a manager if something could be changed. The manager said it couldn't. Whether that's true or not doesn't matter — that answer becomes a 3-star review. The right answer is "let me see what I can do" and then go figure it out. Never tell a guest no without trying. Manager touches are problem-solving moments, not problem-confirming moments.
3
The Check-Back Is the Pattern, Not the Exception
Multiple reviews this week, multiple stores: server asked how the food was before guests started eating. Server offered to-go boxes for unfinished food and never asked if anything was wrong. Server pushed for entrée orders without offering apps or water. Same complaint, week after week. Two-bite check-back. Every table. Every shift. We've said this. Now we do it.
