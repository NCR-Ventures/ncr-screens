NCR Ventures 3 Opportunities to Be Better Week Ending 5.17.26 — Mother's Day Held, Andre Owned Easton
5 of 5
1
The First-Impression Pause
Multiple reviews this week describe being seated, then waiting too long before anyone introduces themselves. Guests notice when tables seated after them get greeted first. The host hands off, but the server has to close the loop within 90 seconds. Every time.
2
Course Pacing on Apps and Bread
Bread arriving after entrees. Apps cleared while still half-eaten. Entrees dropping while the table is still on cocktails. Course flow is one of the few service details guests almost always remember. Slow down. Read the table. Don't let the kitchen window dictate the pace of the meal.
3
Salad Composition and Pick
Two separate reviews this week called out salads — browned lettuce, an overload of shredded cheese smothering a Caesar. Salads are the easiest dish to make right and the easiest one to phone in. Pick line standards, ingredient checks before plating, and a manager taste at the start of every shift.
